Toolbox Mount

Custom stainless toolbox spacers!

pasted directly from journal:
I will come back and put in images and properly document later but I want to get this up first.

I was given an old truck toolbox as a gift from my old electrician boss. (I organized our storage unit and there were 3 of them taking up space) but unfortunately the best one just barely fit my truck in width, and definitely did not fit in depth.

There's a lot of different solutions to this problem, and if I wanted to solve it cheaply I'd just use wood and some bar stock, or weld onto the box, but I hadn't done any personal projects through sendcutsend yet and figured this was as good a time as any.

I initially planned to clamp in front of and behind the box, but after measuring how far the lid swings back I realized I could clamp to the inside of my truck rail (there's a spot for racks to insert to the body) so I did that.

--

Detailing my thought process on this project for posterity

Requirements:

  • offset toolbox vertically
  • mount the toolbox securely without permanent modifications

Alternative solutions to this problem:

  • I don't actually need to do this, there's a smaller toolbox that fits my truck that I could swap this one for in my old boss's storage unit (which he let me keep the key for). I prefer this one for a few reasons (cleaner, bigger, matches truck color better).
  • If I was broke and needed the toolbox mounted I'd just do this with box tubing and a flat sheet that I hacksawed (or the unistrut I have lying around).
  • Welding on clamp points to the side.
  • Bolting on clamp points (L channel)

--

Regardless I left it kicking around in my head for a few days until I'd figured out the design I wanted.
