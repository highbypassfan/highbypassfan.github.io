NPS Research

A summary of 2 summers interning at Naval Postgraduate school.

I spent two summers at NPS during high school. It was my first proper job outside of family work and I think it went pretty well! 

Actually a good lesson in getting jobs here too: 

I had been doing some 3D printing on my own for about a year at that point. At the open house for the internship program (students basically all walk around and hand out resumes/talk to profs) I talked to a few spots but made an alright impression with Dr. Luhrs. However the day after I then sent her and a few other spots an email talking about my interest in the project and why I think I'd be a good fit, citing my 3d printing experience. She later told me that that's the reason she hired me. It is your responsibility to follow up!

Regardless, the first internship was in person and was really fun. Basically just tensile testing of maraging steel parts but we used a lot of different hardware. Whenever I got to use the SEM I was stressed out because 1.5 million is a lot of money to not break for a kid haha. I helped heat treat samples, log and mount composite plastic samples in a UV machine, and mount metal ones in the salt fog machine. I also helped another professor design a jig for her graphene research and printed it on my home printer. I was ahead of schedule so I also designed some hardness testing samples for the titanium printer we had on site. That was really fun to watch/help with. Then polished the samples and tested their hardness along the edge of the part. 

paper is here: https://www.mdpi.com/2075-4701/10/2/218


My second internship was work from home during Covid. I was designing some phase change material fluid wicks and testing using my printer. I made a good number of iterations but it really was just not the same, way harder to be excited and motivated at home vs in a lab with other people haha. 

Overall though a great experience.
